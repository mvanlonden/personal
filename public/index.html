  2. Welcome!

    I'm Mel

    I am an ambitious person, eager to make an impact.

    Let me tell you a little bit more about myself and my personal philosophy.

    It's all about impact and reach. By multiplying impact and reach we get what I call the "Change Index." Let's go off the assumption that we live and we die; this is the only time we have to do anything. There is nothing after you die. I think it goes without saying that before we leave this earth, we want our opinion to matter. Let's further assume that you have good intentions. Chances are you have an opinion because it will change things for the better. Now how do we value our life? I think the true value should lie in how much we can increase life value for others. As a collective we should value forward progression--find humankind's homeostasis--to reach a point where living becomes second nature, so that we can focus on further expanding the collective intelligence pool. This being the end result, the ever-continuing frontier, let's try to put ourselves in this context--we get our Personal Change Index. The summation of All of our change indices creates the Collective Change Index. And our goal is to maximize this value. This begins with maximizing our own value so that we can show others and future generations how to maximize their own. We stand on the shoulder of giants.

    Intuitively, we would think our impact and reach are inversely related. If one goes up, the other inherently goes down, the underlying limit here being time. This has changed. Using technology we can replicate our efforts with perfect accuracy and precision. Here reach becomes very large. Changing 1s and 0s on our own computers, our work can be replicated to create almost limitless efficiency. Time is no longer the limiting factor. Our impact is now just limited by the resourcefulness of our computerized labor. So that is why I'm here.

      Impact: The extent to which our personal efforts affects others.

      Reach: How far we can extend our impact.

  3. Heart Hub

    Hospital Data Collection and Analytics Platform

    At MIT's H@cking Medicine hosted at WebMD, three teammates and I formulated a plan to create a universal hospital device platform that will be comprised of an access point network for medical devices.

    The device network, dubbed Heart Hub, will provide a hardware protocol API on one end and an analytics API on the other end to make it easy to unify medical device information and other hosptial informatics in a central store. It will promote a data science community by providing a platform for accessing the data seamlessly and securely.

  4. DYML.io

    Dynamic Medication Reconcilation

    After winning the GE/Intel Innovation prize at H@cking Medicine at MIT we are continuing to develop a dynamic medication list that will reconcile medications from multiple datastreams (i.e. BB+, EHRs, manual entry) automatically.

    Not only are we looking to reconcile the medications automatically but also create an inclusive user experience that will remind people to take their medication with a "soft" touch, alert users of possible drug interactions and allow the users to email their doctors regarding medication errors.

    Alligator Tracking

    Command Line Tool built using C++

    Dr. Catherine Carr's lab studies sound localization of many different animals in order to better understand the neuronal circuits behind interaural time difference. The alligators were conditioned to respond to a sound that they have associated with food. By anaylzing their response we can better understand the accuracy of their sound localization mechanisms.

    The application is called and then video file and output data file are passed in as arguments. Using a HSV filter the pink paint on the alligator's back is isolated for tracking.
